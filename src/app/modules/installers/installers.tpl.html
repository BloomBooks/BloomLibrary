The Art of Reading

We also recommend that you download and install the Art Of Reading illustration collection. This free collection contains more than 10,000 line drawings suitable for use in reading materials. Bloom is designed to enable you to search and select illustrations from this collection once you have installed it.

Fonts

Bloom installs SIL’s Andika New Basic font which was designed specifically for easier reading in roman scripts.

You can also use a font you already have installed on your computer, but it needs to be a Unicode font. Note that some fonts don't have built-in italics or bold faces. These will look fine on screen, but will not show bold or italics in print or in PDFs.

SIL offers free Unicode fonts for a variety of needs:

  • Andika, designed for literacy materials, especially for beginning readers. Andika covers a wide range of characters that Andika New Basic does not, but it will not show bold or italics in print or PDF.
  • Annapurna, for languages that use Devanagari script
  • Scheherezade, an extended Arabic script font
  • Abyssinica, an Ethiopic script font (also sometimes called “fidel” script)

If you're having trouble finding a font for your language, check out ScriptSource. 

Typing

Bloom assumes that you already have a way to type your language on your computer. If you need a way to type your language and lack one, check out ScriptSource . On Windows you might consider Keyman, available in a free edition.
